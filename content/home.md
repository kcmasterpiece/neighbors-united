# Neighbors United

We are neighbors in the Emeryville, Oakland, and Berkeley area who have united to keep our community livable. We have come together to influence the approval process currently being undertaken by the City of Emeryville of the Sutter Health hospital proposal.

The proposal, unveiled in April 2026, proposes a 330-foot, 17 story, 325 bed, level 1 trauma center with helipad. The operation of the campus, which also includes several other buildings in that location already built, would generate excessive traffic and noise, including helicopters, ambulances, shuttles and cars. It is estimated that the hospital would generate > 20,000 vehicle trips per day. It would greatly compromise our quality of life, including during construction, which is expected to take 4 years. In addition, the project would eliminate a bike boulevard, remove 16 street trees, block the sunlight for residents to the east, and create a dangerous traffic condition for the primary pick up/drop off location for the Emeryville school district.
